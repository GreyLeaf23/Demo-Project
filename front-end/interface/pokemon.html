3 Card Types


You’ll find 3 different types of cards in the Pokémon TCG:


1) Pokémon

  Pokémon cards are the main cards of a deck, which are used to attack your opponent’s Pokémon. The game revolves around the Pokémon your deck has.


2) Energy Cards

  Most of the time, Pokémon can’t attack without Energy cards! You’ll need to match the symbols of the attack cost to the Energy card, but any type of Energy can be used for Colorless Energy.


3) Trainer Cards

  If the deck is the vehicle, the trainer cards are the engine. These cards allow you to do a multitude of things in your turn, which allows you to speed up the process of getting energy cards, tools, and evolutions. They are very diverse in terms of uses and will determine how good your deck is.



Energy types:

  Pokémon need energy to use their attacks and/or abilities. There are 9 different types:

    • Fire

    • Water

    • Leaf

    • Thunder

    • Fighting

    • Steel

    • Psychic

    • Dark

    • Fairy



Parts of every Pokémon Card:


Pokémon Cards:

  Pokémon Type: This determines the type to apply when attacking.

  Hp: This is the innate maximum amount of health points a Pokémon card has.

  Stage: The current stage of evolution, it goes in this order: Basic, Stage 1 and Stage 2.

  Evolves From: Non-Basic Pokémon tell you which Pokémon needs to be in play for them to be placed on the board.

  Ability: These are unique effects that only some card possess.

  Attacks: Moves the Pokémon can use.

  Retreat Cost: How many Energy Cards attached to this Pokémon will need to be discarded to leave the active spot.

  Weakness & Resistance: The damage received by the Pokémon from an attack of that typing will be doubled or halfed respectively.


Trainer Cards:

  Card Name: The name of the card.

  Trainer Type: The type of card it is, these include: Item, Supporters, Stadium and Pokémon tools.

  Effect: This is what the card will do when played.



Playing the Game


How to Win the Game


You can win the game in 3 ways:

  1) Obtain all of your Prize cards.

  2) Knock Out all of your opponent’s Pokémon in play.

  3) If your opponent has no cards left in their deck at the beginning of their turn.


Setting Up to Play


1) Flip a coin. The winner of the coin flip decides which player goes first.

2) Shuffle your 60-card deck and draw the top 7 cards.

3) Check to see if you have any Basic Pokémon in your hand.

4) Put one of your Basic Pokémon face down as your Active Pokémon.

5) Put up to 5 more Basic Pokémon face down on your Bench.

  • If you don’t have any Basic Pokémon, you will do a mulligan:

     Reveal your hand to your opponent.

     Shuffle your hand back into the deck.

     Draw 7 cards.

     Repeat until you have a basic Pokémon.

     And for each mulligan, your opponent can draw an additional card.

6) Put the top 6 cards of your deck off to the side face down as your Prize cards.

7) Both players flip their Active and Benched Pokémon face up and start the game!



Parts of a Turn


Each turn has 3 main parts:


1) Draw a card.


2) Do any of the following actions in any order:

  A. Put Basic Pokémon cards from your hand onto your Bench (as many as you want, up to 5).

  B. Evolve your Pokémon (as many as you want) (you may not evolve a Pokémon that was placed in the same turn).

  C. Attach an Energy card from your hand to one of your Pokémon (once per turn).

  D. Play Trainer cards (as many as you want, but only one Supporter card and one Stadium card per turn).

  E. Retreat your Active Pokémon (only once per turn).

  F. Use Abilities (as many as you want).


3) Attack. Then, end your turn.



Pokémon Checkup and Status Conditions

1) Poisoned

2) Burned

3) Asleep

4) Paralyzed


If a card played states that its effect happens during Pokémon Checkup (or between turns) if stated in the card. You can check Special Conditions in the order of: Other Effects (card effects) then Special Conditions or in reverse. But you can’t mix them, i.e. Special Conditions, Other Effects & Special Conditions.

If a Pokémon during Pokémon Checkup gets knocked out, the player must move a new Pokémon to the Active Spot and the opponent takes the appropriate amount of Prize cards.



Confused

Flip the card upside down. When attacking, you must flip a coin. If its heads, the attack works normally. If its tails, the attack fails, and you put 3 damage counters on the Pokémon.


Paralyzed/Asleep

Turn the card clockwise. The Pokémon cannot attack or retreat (unless it retreats through an item, trainer card, or the effect of an ability on another Pokémon card). After the turn is over, it recovers from the effect.


Poisoned/Burned

Put a Poison/Burn marker on the card. During Pokémon Checkup, put a damage counter on the Pokémon. The card cannot be afflicted with multiple Poison/Burn markers. The new Poison/Burn will replace the old one.


Removing Special Conditions

It can be removed by evolving the Pokémon or moving it to the Bench.

A Pokémon can only be either Asleep, Paralyzed, or Confused this is due to all of these effects turning the card. The last one to be afflicted on the Pokémon is the one that will take effect. Poison and Burn do stack. Thus, a Pokémon to be Burned, Poison and Asleep all at the same time.
